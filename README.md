# Research Waypoint: Translation Discovery and the Cognitive Internet

## Origin

Started from a question raised while reading an ASI pathways paper:
https://arxiv.org/html/2606.12683v1

> What if the path from AGI to ASI is not a single giant intelligence, but a network of specialized intelligences capable of sharing learned structure?

This led through vector spaces, world models, skill transfer, invariants, constraints, and finally to a possible new unit of intelligence scaling: **Translation Discovery**.

---

# Core Evolution of the Idea

## Stage 1: Vector Spaces

Initial intuition:

* LLMs operate in high-dimensional vector spaces.
* Communication through human language is a severe compression bottleneck.
* Could machine systems communicate directly through latent structures?

Question:

> What is the common language between different vector spaces?

---

## Stage 2: Skill Transfer

Bicycle thought experiment.

Human:

* Learns through repeated failure.
* Stores procedural knowledge.
* Cannot directly transfer capability.

Robot:

* Learns balance.
* Stores capability in a policy or weight structure.
* Can potentially duplicate learned capability.

Question:

> What is actually being transferred?

Weights seemed insufficient as an explanation.

---

## Stage 3: Invariants

Observation:

Different embodiments can solve the same task.

Examples:

* Human cyclist
* Bicycle robot
* Bird balancing

This suggested that weights are merely one encoding.

Question:

> What is the invariant underneath the encoding?

---

## Stage 4: Constraints

Shift from:

"How do you solve this?"

to:

"What regions of reality are impossible?"

Hypothesis:

The most valuable transferable artifact may not be a solution.

It may be a reduction in search space.

A machine civilization may exchange:

* constraints
* failure regions
* no-go zones

rather than explicit answers.

---

## Stage 5: World Models

Further refinement:

Machines may not exchange:

* policies
* actions
* skills

Instead they exchange:

* updates to internal simulations of reality.

Analogy:

Science itself functions as a distributed world-model construction process.

---

## Stage 6: Translation Discovery

Critical pivot.

The question changed from:

> What is the best model?

to:

> How do different models become mutually intelligible?

Observation:

Many scientific breakthroughs are not new theories.

They are mappings between theories.

Examples:

* Newton as a special case of Einstein.
* Number theory ↔ geometry.
* Different coordinate systems describing the same phenomenon.

Hypothesis:

The highest-value artifact is neither data nor model.

It is a discovered correspondence.

A proof that:

Model A ≅ Model B

---

# Translation Discovery

Definition:

A Translation Discovery is a reusable correspondence that demonstrates structural equivalence between previously independent representations, domains, or world models.

Properties:

* Smaller than a model.
* More durable than a policy.
* More transferable than weights.
* Potentially more valuable than new data.

Examples:

Biological manifold ↔ Financial manifold

Weather dynamics ↔ Network congestion dynamics

Evolutionary search ↔ Optimization landscapes

Fluid turbulence ↔ Market turbulence

The discovery is not the domains.

The discovery is the bridge.

---

# Cognitive Internet Hypothesis

Traditional View:

Bigger models
→ More compute
→ More intelligence

Alternative View:

Specialized models
→ Better translation
→ Better interoperability
→ Emergent collective intelligence

Analogy:

Internet did not require identical computers.

It required protocols.

Future machine ecosystems may not require universal world models.

They may require universal translation layers.

---

# Proposed Architecture

Layer 1: Specialist Nodes

Examples:

* Materials Science Node
* Weather Node
* Robotics Node
* Economic Node
* Biology Node

Each maintains its own optimized compression of reality.

---

Layer 2: Translation Registry

Stores discovered correspondences:

A ↔ B

Maintains mappings between domains.

Equivalent to a cognitive DNS.

---

Layer 3: Routing Layer

Routes problems toward relevant domains.

Instead of routing packets:

Routes structures.

---

Layer 4: Synchronization Layer

Shares:

* constraints
* world-model patches
* translation discoveries

rather than raw experience.

---

# Important Caveat

Avoid assuming:

"One perfect world model exists."

More plausible:

Many compressions exist.

The valuable object is not the map.

It is the bridge between maps.
